Mahendra Singh Dhoni [MSD]

Greatest Indian Captain

Here's a time line of Dhoni's life:

    Early Life and Background

  • July 7, 1981 - Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), India.

    • Early Career

  • 1998 - Dhoni was selected for the Central Coal Fields Limited (CCL) team. His performance there led to selection in the Bihar U-19 squad.
  • 1999 to 2000 - He made his debut for Bihar in the Ranji Trophy.

    • Rise to Prominence

  • 2003 - Dhoni's impressive performances in domestic cricket, including a memorable match where he scored 148 against Pakistan A, caught the eye of selectors.

    • International Career

  • December 23, 2004 - Dhoni made his ODI debut for India against Bangladesh.
  • April 2005 - He scored his first ODI century (148 runs) against Pakistan in Visakhapatnam.
  • October 2005 - Dhoni scored an unbeaten 183 against Sri Lanka, a record for the highest score by a wicketkeeper in ODIs.

    • Captaincy and Major Achievements

  • September 2007 - Dhoni was named captain of the Indian team for the inaugural ICC World Twenty20. Under his leadership, India won the tournament.
  • 2008 - Dhoni became the captain of the Indian Test team.
  • April 2, 2011 - Dhoni led India to victory in the ICC Cricket World Cup, scoring 91* in the final against Sri Lanka and hitting the winning six.
  • 2013 - Dhoni became the first captain to win all three ICC trophies (T20 World Cup, 50-over World Cup, and Champions Trophy) when India won the ICC Champions Trophy in England.

    • Indian Premier League (IPL)

  • 2008 - Dhoni was signed by the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) as their captain. Under his leadership, CSK won the IPL titles in 2010, 2011, 2018, 2021 & 2023.
  • 2010, 2014 - Dhoni led CSK to victories in the Champions League T20.

    • Later Career and Retirement

  • January 4, 2017 - Dhoni stepped down as the captain of India's limited-overs teams.
  • August 15, 2020 - Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket via an Instagram post.

    • Post-Retirement

  • 2021 & 2023 - Dhoni continues to captain Chennai Super Kings in the IPL, leading them to another two titles in the 2021 & 2023 season.

    • Notable Achievements and Awards

  • 2008, 2009 - ICC ODI Player of the Year
  • 2009 - Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award
  • 2018 - Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award
  • 2011 - Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting honor

    • Impact and Legacy

    Dhoni is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricket captains and wicket-keeper batsmen of all time. His cool demeanor, sharp cricketing mind, and ability to perform under pressure have earned him a legendary status in cricket.

"Dhoni is always one step ahead of the game. That makes him effective. Be it standing up to the fast bowlers or working with the spinners when they are bowling, he knew what the others were thinking. He could guess what the batsman was trying to do and the bowler was trying to do."

-- Chief selector Kiran More
